My Story

From Photoshop Layers
to AI-Powered Products

This is not a standard cover letter. It is a short honest account of how I got here — and why I am genuinely motivated to build a career in the digital space, whether that means web development, AI tools, graphic design, or data analytics. I am open to the right opportunity, wherever it is.

It started with layers

My first real encounter with digital tools came when I was still in primary school. A friend showed me Photoshop — specifically how layers work — and something clicked. The idea that you could build an image from invisible stacked pieces, control every element independently, and create something that looked seamless but was actually deeply structured: that idea has never really left me.

Around the same time I discovered GTA San Andreas Multiplayer and became curious about how the online servers worked. I found my way to server files and a scripting environment called Pawno. I wasn't a programmer — I want to be honest about that — but I was tinkering, reading, experimenting, and learning that digital systems have logic you can understand if you're patient enough to look.

A proper foundation in design

I studied Graphic Design and Print Media at Spojená škola Kremnička in Banská Bystrica. The programme covered everything from typography and layout to Adobe tools, colour theory, and the basics of visual communication. We also touched on web design concepts and introductory programming.

That education gave me something practical and lasting: I learned how to think visually, how to make design decisions that have a reason behind them, and how to care about craft and detail. Those habits apply to everything I build today — even when the output is code or a data dashboard.

Life takes turns — and that's fine

After school I didn't go straight into a tech or design job. I needed to work, and I took what was available. Warehouse logistics in Slovakia and the Netherlands. A stint on event stages doing sound, LED panels, and VJing. Solar panel installation. And for the last couple of years, bartending at a mountain resort in Norway.

None of those jobs are what I want my career to be. But I don't regret them either. Every one of them taught me something real: discipline under pressure, adaptability, reliability, how to work hard in uncomfortable conditions, how to function in international environments, and how to keep a good attitude even when things are difficult. I also kept doing graphic and design work on the side throughout most of this period — it was never something I fully walked away from.

Then AI changed the picture

I started paying close attention to AI tools earlier than most people around me. I was experimenting with early Midjourney before it became mainstream. I watched how these tools were evolving and I understood fairly quickly that this was not a trend — this was a fundamental shift in what a motivated, tool-literate person could build without a traditional engineering background.

Over the last year and a half I deliberately invested serious time into this. I learned how to actually build web applications using Next.js, Supabase, Vercel, and TypeScript — not perfectly, not like a senior engineer, but enough to ship things that genuinely work. I created a restaurant data dashboard, an AI agent and workflow tool, a multilingual agency website with voice AI integration, and a real-time multiplayer quiz game. Each project taught me something the previous one didn't.

I actively use OpenAI, Claude, Cursor, Krea.ai, Midjourney, GitHub, and Make.com in my daily workflow. I understand how to prompt effectively, how to structure AI-assisted development, how to use these tools to move fast and build things that look and feel good.

What I'm looking for now

I am not applying for a senior engineering position. I know exactly where I am in my learning: junior level, actively growing, genuinely motivated. What I bring to a team is not years of professional experience in software development — it is design sensibility, practical AI fluency, fast learning, and a real track record of shipping independent digital projects from scratch.

I am looking for a role in AI tools, web development, data support, automation, or digital product work where I can contribute meaningfully from day one and keep developing over time. I work well independently, I take ownership of what I build, and I am the kind of person who figures things out rather than waiting to be told exactly what to do.

If you are building something in the AI or digital space and you need someone who combines design thinking, practical tool knowledge, strong visual instincts, and genuine curiosity — I would love to talk.
